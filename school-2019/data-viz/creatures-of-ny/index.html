I am always fascinated by non-human beings living in New York. I am a dog owner, so naturally I love seeing dogs prance around the city. Recently there was a bunch of goats in my favorite park, and who doesn't love that?. I'm tickled every time I see a raccoon. My data set in this case in 311 data, so I'll be looking at complaints about these creatures. I wanted to know which animals got under New Yorkers' skin.

On my block rats constantly scamper across the sidewalks at night. I never see it as bad in other neighborhoods. I wanted to know if we were alone. Some preliminary data, looking only at 2018 showed that my zip code (10025) had the most rodent complaints of all zip coddes in New York. I then got competitive and wanted to know who the other offenders were and how the number of complaints has changed over time. For example, if I see that they are coming down, the situation might actually be getting better. To my dismay, the situation in my zip code has actually gotten much worse as the years have gone on.

I am particularly prone to bug bites, and a few years ago I was reading articles of mosquito infestations in the city. The best defense is to not have any standing water. This got me wondering if there was a relationship between standing water complaints and Mosquito complaints. There doesn't seem to be at all, which I found interesting in and of itself.
